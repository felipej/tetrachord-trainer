Any way of adding sound fonts or something like that? 3:36 PM Great question. There are a few approaches, ranked from most practical to most ambitious: SoundFonts via WebAudio — the sweet spot. There's a library called soundfont-player that loads pre-rendered instrument samples from a CDN (Benjamin Gleitzman's hosted set from FluidR3 and MusyngKite soundfonts). It gives you acoustic piano, electric piano, vibraphone, guitar, etc. — all real sampled instruments, loaded on demand. The catch: it's an external dependency, so the app would need to fetch samples on first load (a few hundred KB per instrument). But it works entirely client-side. Tone.js Sampler. Tone.js (which is available in Claude artifacts) has a Sampler class that can load individual audio samples mapped to pitches and interpolate between them. You could point it at any hosted sample set. This is essentially a DIY soundfont approach with more control. WebAudio FM synthesis — better synth, no samples.
